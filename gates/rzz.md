The $R_{zz}$ gate implements an Ising-type interaction with rotation angle $\theta$.

$$
R_{zz}(\theta) = \exp\left(-i\frac{\theta}{2}Z\otimes Z\right) =
\begin{bmatrix}
    e^{-i\frac{\theta}{2}} & 0                     & 0                     & 0 \\\\
    0                      & e^{i\frac{\theta}{2}} & 0                     & 0 \\\\
    0                      & 0                     & e^{i\frac{\theta}{2}} & 0 \\\\
    0                      & 0                     & 0                     & e^{-i\frac{\theta}{2}}
\end{bmatrix}
$$

### Properties

- Diagonal and entangling for $\theta$ not a multiple of $\pi$.
- Can be implemented with two CNOTs and a single-qubit $R_z$ rotation.
- Generated by an Ising $Z\otimes Z$ coupling.

### Usage

- Native entangling gate in Ising-type hardware.
- Common in QAOA and Hamiltonian simulation.
